ramen

Ramen originated in China as wheat noodle soups and was introduced to Japan by Chinese immigrants in the late 19th century. Its name derives from the Chinese lamian (pulled noodles). Initially a cheap, filling street food called "Chinese soba", it exploded in popularity after World War II and was later revolutionized globally by the invention of instant ramen.


ramen recipe

ingredients

  • Ramen noodles
  • Garlic and ginger
  • Broth (chicken or vegetarian)
  • Dried shiitake mushrooms
  • Veggies like carrots or kale
  • All your favorite toppings like some panko, egg, chili oil, etc.

directions

  1. Stirfry garlic and ginger.
  2. Add some chicken broth and dried shiitake mushrooms for some umami punch
  3. Cook your noodles right in the broth with some scallions
  4. Add thinly sliced kale, shredded carrots, whatever you'd like! Cook until just tender.
  5. Add some crunchy panko crumbs, a soft-boiled egg, chili oil, hot sauce, sesame oil, and/or soy sauce, whatever your heart desires.
  6. Serve hot and enjoy!
